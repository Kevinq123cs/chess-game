Genset spending
The generator (genset) slice of data-center capex, sized two ways below — top-down and bottom-up — that both land at ≈1% of capex. Donut center = each year's AI-attributable total; slices split it by category. The ~1% shares are editable assumptions (source range 0.8–1.2%) — tweak to see the pies move.
Top-down
genset $ = AIDC capex × genset-intensity (~1%)
Works because total capex and the genset market are both published, so one reliable ratio sizes the whole pie fast.
Intensity ≈ generator market ÷ capex: $10.3bn ÷ $927.5bn ≈ 1.1% (2026). Sources [1][2]; capex = tier totals above.
Bottom-up
genset $ = capacity (GW) × backup ratio (~1–2×) × $/MW
Works because it counts physical hardware once per facility, so it sidesteps the T1/T2 financing double-count. ~10 GW/yr new build × ~$1bn/GW ≈ ~$10bn (2026).
$/MW ≈ Cummins ~$350/kW mission-critical [3]; install base 20→55 GW (2018→2024) [4].
Honesty note: the ~1% genset-intensity and ~1–2× backup ratio are rules-of-thumb derived by combining the cited inputs (generator market, capex, $/MW, GW), not lifted from any single citation.
Categories are owner-built capex (T1, T2, T3a China) — additive and clean, since each entity capitalizes what it builds. Colo (Equinix/DLR) is deliberately excluded — it's a landlord whose facilities house the same hyperscaler tenants already in T1/T2, so counting it would double-count. We'd rather under- than over-count. Edit a share to update the pies; Reset restores the originals.
